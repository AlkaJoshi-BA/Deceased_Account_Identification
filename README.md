Project Title:<br>
Case Study on Identification of Deceased Accounts in Banks<br>
Business Problem:<br>
Commercial banks manage a diverse range of customer accounts, including salaried individuals and pensioners. One critical operational challenge arises when a customer passes away.<br>
There is often a time gap between the death of a customer, the bank being formally notified, and the account being marked or suspended.<br>
This delay creates a vulnerability window where unauthorized or unethical transactions may occur in the deceased customer's account.<br>
Despite existing controls, this remains a persistent issue in the banking sector, leading to operational inefficiencies, increased risk of fraud, and compliance concerns.<br>
This project analyzes this gap and identifies opportunities to improve the process and reduce associated risks.<br>
Objective:<br>
*Analyze the delay between customer death notification and account action
*Identify process gaps and inefficiencies
*Highlight potential risk points in the workflow
*Suggest improvements to reduce fraud and compliance risks<br>
Key Focus Areas:<br>
*Account monitoring for inactive or high-risk profiles
*Notification and verification workflows
*Turnaround time for account suspension
*Risk exposure during the delay period
