The following stakeholders are integral to the process of identifying deceased customers and managing their bank accounts. Each stakeholder plays a specific role in ensuring that due diligence has been conducted on these accounts and transaction restrictions have been made. 
•	Customer’s family or nominee - Informs the bank about the death of the customer. Submit death certificate and proper documents for claiming the amount present in the account.
•	Head of the Branch - Receives the information and conducts due diligence for verification of the document.
•	Compliance department - Ensures regulatory procedures are followed
•	Bank IT system or Staff - Updates customer status and restricts account transactions
•	Government registry - Maintains official records of registered deaths
